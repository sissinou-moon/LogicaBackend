## Hardest Equation in the World

The **Navier-Stokes existence and smoothness** problem is often considered one of the hardest equations in the world. It is a set of partial differential equations that describe the motion of viscous fluid substances.

- **Millennium Prize Problem**: It is one of the seven **Millennium Prize Problems** selected by the Clay Mathematics Institute.
- **Unsolved**: Despite decades of research, it remains unsolved.
- **Equation**: The Navier-Stokes equations in their incompressible form are:
  \[
  \frac{\partial \mathbf{u}}{\partial t} + (\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla)\mathbf{u} = -\nabla p + \nu \Delta \mathbf{u} + \mathbf{f}
  \]
  where \(\mathbf{u}\) is the velocity field, \(p\) is pressure, \(\nu\) is kinematic viscosity, and \(\mathbf{f}\) is an external force.

This equation is crucial for understanding turbulence, weather patterns, and fluid dynamics in engineering.
